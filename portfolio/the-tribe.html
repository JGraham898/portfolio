The Tribe

The Project

THE TRIBE is a brand new app designed for people involved in start-up accelerator programs. It allows founders, program organisors, investors and mentors to communicate, network, keep track of events and attendees, and advertise their businesses.

The Problem

The founder of THE TRIBE informed me that he wanted to offer a founder-first approach - in other words, his priority was to provide an excellent user experience for the start-up founders using the app.

However, as the website was set up without any initial UX research, there were some areas of confusion which needed to be addressed.

My Role

I set out to redesign the community functionality features of the app. The goal was to help users network, upload information about their business, and find the information they need in a seamless, intuitive and aesthetically pleasing way.

As the sole UX designer, I led the entire project. I conducted research and user testing, came up with design ideas, and created Figma wireframes.

User Interviews

The app initally had a minimum viable product (MVP) release that had been built by the engineers without any UX design input. My first step was to conduct interviews with 5 users in the primary focus group (startup founders).

My goals were:

  • Empathise with user needs
  • Discover pain points in current user journeys
  • Measure user satisfaction with the current experience

During the interviews, I observed them interacting with the app/website and narrating their thoughts.

I asked them to go through the following user journeys:

  • Editing their personal profile
  • Editing their venture profile page and pitch deck
  • Finding and contacting other founders
  • Using the filter to find ventures by name and industry

Gathering Insights

After the interviews, I carried out affinity mapping to identify users' key pain points.

I then created a list of "How Might We?" questions to clarify what I needed to achieve. Before proceeding with design sketches, I consulted with The Tribe's founder to identify business factors which might affect my ability to resolve these problems.

Solution Sketching

I created some low fidelity sketches with annotations to express my initial ideas. This involved:

  • Rethinking information heirarchies and layouts
  • Designing a new filtering system
  • Providing more visual cues and information to help users achieve their goals

Wireframes

Based on my initial sketches, I created some wireframes in Figma.

See Figma wireframes here.

Iterative Testing

Throughout the whole process of sketches and wireframes, I conducted continuous user testing, showing my ideas to users and to THE TRIBE's founder. The feedback on my first wireframes was overall more positive than for the MVP, but I was able to identify some areas where I could hone the design even further.

Next Steps

My work on this project is ongoing; my next steps are to hone my wireframes even further based on user feedback, and meet with THE TRIBE's engineers to gather their input before they go ahead with implementing the designs.
