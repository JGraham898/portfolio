EndorFIT

The Project

As part of my UX course at Interaction Design Foundation, I was tasked with designing a new exercise app to help people stay fit during the coronavirus lockdown. The project was focused on utilising the Design Thinking model:

EMPATHISE > DEFINE > IDEATE > PROTOTYPE > TEST

User Research

I began by preparing a Typeform survey which was answered by 12 participants. The survey had a mixture of open-ended and multiple choice questions.

I wanted to understand:

  • Why do they want to exercise?
  • What are their current exercise habits?
  • What are the obstacles preventing them from exercising?

The survey gave me some interesting quotes and insights:

"I used to be really active in my 20s and I want to get back to that. I feel like less exercise means less energy, flexibility and more health problems."

"My activity levels have dropped during lockdown and I want something that helps me move more."

"I find it difficult to fit exercise into my schedule and reap not just the physical, but the mental health benefits."

"I mostly exercise for mental health reasons."

"Community experience is important for encouragement, especially while we're in lockdown."

Defining My Goals

I created an affinity diagram using the Miro tool to make sense of the data from my survey.

From my diagram, these were the findings that interested me the most:

  • Mental health, community and friendship were important, as well as physical health and fitness
  • Tiredness, lack of time and motivation were the biggest obstacles
  • People exercise in many different locations, depending on what space they have available to them

Personas

From the insights gathered, I created personas to represent archetypal users, in order to visualise how I can best serve those users' needs with my app design.

For each persona, I wrote a short Point Of View statement to sum up their needs in an actionable way.

I also wrote some "How Might We?" questions for each persona to think about how I could best serve their needs with my app design.


PERSONA 1: SAM.

BIO: 27 year old male who works long hours.

NEED: To exercise and spend time outdoors for good mental health.

INSIGHT: The user enjoys exercising with friends, being outdoors and exploring his city, but can find the atmosphere at gyms to be off-putting.

POV: A busy man in his 20s needs to spend time being active and with friends to maintain good mental health.

  • How might we make exercising a social experience to be shared with friends?
  • How might we emphasize the mental health benefits of exercising?
  • How might we help people to spend more time outdoors while exercising?


PERSONA 2: LIAM.

BIO: 34 year old male who works an office job and has a long commute to work.

NEED: To stay active and maintain flexibility, mobility and energy levels.

INSIGHT: The user was very active when he was younger, but the demands of full time work and lockdown mean he is not as active as he once was. He sometimes finds it hard to find motivation, but enjoys exercising with his partner.

POV: An office worker needs to fit exercise into his life to maintain good flexibility and energy levels into his 30s.

  • How might we come up with short exercises which are easy to fit into a busy day?
  • How might we make exercises enjoyable so motivation becomes easier?
  • How might we make exercises accessible for people who are at different fitness levels?


PERSONA 3: MARIA.

BIO: 27 year old female who mostly works from home, spending most of the day at a desk.

NEED: Something to help her stretch and move around when she's stuck at home, particularly during lockdown.

INSIGHT: The user doesn't like to focus on calories or weight loss, but instead on general health and flexibility. She sometimes needs ideas or demos to help her get started with exercise.

POV: A woman in her 20s needs non-weight-loss-focused exercise guidance to maintain overall health and wellbeing.

  • How might we make exercises focused on health and wellbeing rather than weight loss?
  • How might we demonstrate proper exercise techniques to users?
  • How might we give users ideas for exercises or routines that would be right for them?

Initial Ideas

I wanted my app name to not only reflect the health and fitness, but also convey a feeling of positivitya nd good vibes. I brainstormed some ideas with one of my sample users and together we came up with the name ENDORFIT - a combination of "fit" and "endorphin" to convey the idea that the app will help to get users' endorphins pumping and make them feel good.

To come up with UI ideas for Endorfit, I looked at other fitness apps on the market (such as Strava, which was mentioned favourably by some of my users). I identified features which I thought would fulfil my user needs, as well as answering my "How might we?" questions.

This part of my research yielded the following ideas:

  • Fun "medals" and "trophies" to gain when you achieve certain things
  • Connect with friends and give an "exercise together" option
  • Include songs/playlists to suit your workout mood/tempo
  • Provide instructional videos to help people understand how to do the workouts
  • Provide helpful tips on exercise and fitness

User Flows

I created some flowcharts using the Lucid app to visualise the journey the user would take through the app and help me to plan how the different pages of the app will flow into each other.

Prototypes

Working from my user journey flowcharts, I created some quick wireframes in Figma. I then showed these to some of my users to gather their feedback and work out which direction to go in next.
